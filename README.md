# Types of Wireframes

| **Feature**               | **Low-Fidelity Wireframe**                          | **High-Fidelity Wireframe**                         |
|---------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------|
| **Detail level**           | Basic, rough sketches                               | Detailed, close to final design                     |

| **Usage stage**            | Early-stage concept, quick iteration                | Later-stage design, testing, and developer handoff  |

### Scenario: E-Commerce Checkout Flow

A product team at a mid-sized e-commerce company was designing a new multi-step checkout flow. Before any development began, they created **low-fidelity wireframes** to outline each step: Cart, Shipping Info, Payment, and Confirmation.

During a design review session, stakeholders and UX designers noticed that:
- The **"Back" button** was inconsistently placed across steps.
- The **shipping form** asked for unnecessary fields (e.g., second phone number), potentially causing user frustration.
- There was no **clear progress indicator**, which could leave users confused about how many steps were left.

### Resolution

Thanks to the early wireframes:
- The design team **revised the layout**, ensuring consistent button placement across all screens.
- They **simplified the form**, asking only for essential information.
- A **progress bar** was added at the top of each step to improve transparency and guide users.

These changes were implemented **before any code was written**, saving significant time and resources that would have been spent reworking the design later.

### Impact

When the final product launched:
- Drop-off rates in the checkout flow decreased by **22%**.
- Users reported a smoother, more intuitive experience.
- The development team reported **fewer design-related bugs** and better clarity during implementation.
